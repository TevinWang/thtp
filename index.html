Our Courses

The Human Tech Project's variety of coding and technology related courses, perfectly suited for any elementary or middle schooler.

An Introduction to Raspberry Pi and Electronics

This 6-week program gives middle school students a brief introduction to components that we take for granted. This program delves into the workings of components like capacitors, inductors, resistors, etc. Students will put together simple circuits in order to better understand the power of the devices they take for granted (tablets, PCs, etc.)

Recommended Age: Middle School

Getting Started with Technology

This ebook is designed to teach seniors how their devices work. It covers basic content including email and creating social networking accounts to bridge the gap between seniors and their grandchildren, the youngest generation.

Recommended Age: Senior

Introduction to Computer Programming with Python

This 4-week summer program gives elementary and middle school students an introduction to programming. Python is a great language for anyone looking to get into coding. We teach the basics of Python and prepare students to start making their own projects.

Recommended Ages: Elementary/Middle School

An Introduction to C++

This 6-week program gives middle school students an introduction in the famous coding language, C++. Students will learn syntax and start to build some cool projects.

Recommended Ages: Middle School

Web Development using HTML/CSS/JS

This 4-week program will help students learn how to create websites. We teach JavaScript, HTML, and CSS, preparing students to start their own basic website projects.

Recommended Ages: Middle School

Introduction to Firebase

This 4-week program will give students an introduction to Firebase. Firebase is Google's mobile platform that helps people quickly develop high-quality apps and grow their businesses.

Recommended Ages: Middle School

About The Human Tech Project

'The Human Tech Project' is a non-for-profit organization which uses the technical skills and experience of the youth for the betterment of the world. Its primary beneficiaries are technologically challenged seniors and underprivileged children. As we move into the age of the Internet, it is becoming more and more important for every individual to embrace the convenience technology brings. That is why we are teaching a rapidly expanding amount of basic technology and programming courses across many locations. Feel free to contact us to request a class!

Hackathons


Cuper Hacks is a student led, nonprofit 12 hour hackathon for high schoolers that primarily focuses on encouraging coders of all skill levels to innovate and create programs that can revolutionize and improve lives. Cuper Hacks intends to create an environment for students of all skill levels in the coding field to come and innovate using their creativity.



PlatHacks is a online, global hackathon for highschoolers that focuses on using coding to create projects that attempt to solve any large societal problem, by intertwining CS with fields like biology, medicine, business and politics. This Hackathon is completely FREE, thanks to our wonderful sponsors, and no coding experience is required.



ThinkHacks is 28 hour hackathon designed for high school students to engineer, program, learn, and have fun! They do so in an environment where people can connect, communicate, exchange knowledge, and most importantly bring their dreams to life at ThinkHacks I.

Frequently Ask Questions

What if my child has never coded before?

That is completely fine! We are here to help children get into coding and robotics, and no previous experience is required.

What if my childer is older than the recommended ages?

That is alright! It is never too late to start coding, and we will tailor our courses to help your child to the best of our ability.

Are there any underlying costs?

Nope! These courses are completely free!

How are you guys qualified?

The Human Tech Project's team consists of many experienced students who have taken college level computer science courses as well participate in the USA Coding Olympiad, Robotics, and other CS related competitions. They've all seen large success in all of their endeavors and are excited to help the next generation get involved with CS and Robotics.
